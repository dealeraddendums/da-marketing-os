We've talked to hundreds of dealer F&I managers and sales desk staff over the years. When we ask how long it takes to print an addendum for a new vehicle, the answers range from "about 15 minutes" to "honestly, sometimes half an hour if we have to track down all the product info."

That time adds up. For a dealer selling 100 units a month, 15 minutes per vehicle is 25 hours of staff time — every single month — spent on paperwork.

Here's how dealers using DealerAddendums get that down to 30 seconds or less.

## Why Manual Addendums Take So Long

Before we talk solutions, let's be specific about where the time actually goes:

1. **Looking up vehicle details** — pulling the sticker price, finding the exact trim, confirming the package contents
2. **Selecting the right products** — figuring out which F&I products apply to this specific vehicle (some dealers exclude certain products from base model vehicles, for example)
3. **Entering dealer add-ons** — typing in paint protection, nitrogen tires, window tint, floor mats with the right prices
4. **Formatting** — making sure it looks professional, has the right logo, the right font
5. **Printing, verifying, reprinting** — because someone typed the price wrong, or the logo is blurry, or the form cut off

Every one of these steps is a candidate for automation. And automating all of them is exactly what the 30-second addendum looks like.

## Step 1: Connect Your Inventory

The biggest time sink is looking up vehicle information manually. The fix is automatic inventory import.

With DealerAddendums, we pull your inventory directly from your website feed, your DMS, or your syndication provider. When a new vehicle lands on your lot, it's in the system within hours — automatically. VIN, year, make, model, trim, MSRP, and options are already populated.

Your F&I manager doesn't type a single vehicle detail. They find the vehicle, click print.

If you're a [franchise dealer](/lp/ford-dealers) with a DMS integration, inventory is even faster — typically same-day from your DMS push.

## Step 2: Set Product Rules Once

This is the step that turns a 15-minute process into a 30-second one.

DealerAddendums lets you set automatic product rules based on any combination of:
- Year
- Make
- Model
- Trim
- Body style
- Mileage

Here's what that looks like in practice: you tell the system "apply paint protection and door edge guards to all new vehicles" and "apply nitrogen tire inflation to all vehicles with MSRP over $30,000." You set it once. It runs forever.

When a vehicle matches a rule, the products are added automatically. Your team never has to manually select F&I products for a vehicle again. The addendum is already built — they just hit print.

For [used car dealers](/lp/used-car-dealers), the same logic applies: set rules for reconditioning packages, CPO products, or AS-IS language based on mileage bands.

## Step 3: Hard Adds vs. Soft Adds

One of the more nuanced addendum decisions is how you display products that are already installed versus products that are dealer-offered options.

Hard adds (installed products like protective coatings or accessories already on the vehicle) should be combined with the MSRP for transparency. Soft adds (optional products like extended warranties or paint protection packages that aren't pre-installed) are listed separately.

DealerAddendums supports both formats with separate templates. You configure which products are hard vs. soft once, and the right template is used automatically. No decision for your team to make at print time.

## Step 4: Mobile Printing for the Lot

Your F&I manager sits at a desk. But vehicles aren't always near a desk — they're on the lot, in the service drive, arriving from auction.

The DA Installer iOS app lets your team scan a VIN barcode, QR code, or type in a stock number from anywhere on the lot. The vehicle loads instantly from your inventory. The addendum is built automatically based on your rules. Print it from the app or queue it for desktop printing.

No walking inside, no looking up the vehicle, no manual entry. Scan and print.
