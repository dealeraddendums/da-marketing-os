Running a franchise dealership means satisfying two sets of compliance requirements at once: your OEM's standards and the FTC's consumer protection rules. They overlap in some areas and diverge in others. Getting either one wrong can mean dealer agreement violations, FTC fines, or both.

This checklist covers the documentation compliance items that affect franchise dealers daily. It's designed for your F&I director, compliance officer, or general manager to review quarterly.

## New Vehicle Compliance

### Window Sticker (Monroney Label)

- [ ] Every new vehicle on the lot displays the federally required Monroney label
- [ ] The label is affixed to the vehicle window (not placed on the dash or seat)
- [ ] MSRP, destination charges, and all optional equipment are accurately listed
- [ ] The label has not been removed or obscured — federal law prohibits removal by anyone except the purchaser

### Dealer Addendum

- [ ] A dealer addendum is displayed on every new vehicle alongside the Monroney label
- [ ] The addendum clearly identifies all dealer-installed accessories and their prices
- [ ] Dealer mark-ups above MSRP are disclosed on the addendum (not buried in the finance paperwork)
- [ ] The addendum uses your current dealer logo and branding
- [ ] Hard adds (pre-installed products combined with MSRP) are clearly distinguished from soft adds
- [ ] Product descriptions are specific — no vague line items like "dealer prep" without itemization

### OEM Compliance

- [ ] Addendum templates are reviewed against your OEM's addendum guidelines (check your dealer agreement annually)
- [ ] F&I products listed on the addendum are authorized under your OEM's program
- [ ] Pricing displayed is consistent with what appears in your DMS and what the customer is charged

## Used Vehicle Compliance

### FTC Buyers Guide

- [ ] Every used vehicle offered for sale displays a current FTC Buyers Guide
- [ ] The form version is the 2024 revision (updated format with airbag recall disclosure)
- [ ] The correct warranty type is checked: As-Is, Implied Warranty, or Full/Limited Dealer Warranty
- [ ] If providing a warranty, all covered systems are listed individually — no vague "powertrain" without specifics
- [ ] The airbag safety recall disclosure box is completed accurately
- [ ] If any negotiation or sale is conducted in Spanish, a Spanish-language Buyers Guide is provided
- [ ] The buyer signs and receives a copy of the Buyers Guide at time of sale
- [ ] Your copy is retained with the deal jacket

### Used Vehicle Addendum / Window Sticker

- [ ] Used vehicles have a sticker or form displaying the asking price prominently
- [ ] Dealer-added products (tint, protection packages, GPS) are individually disclosed and priced
- [ ] Reconditioning costs are disclosed if they're being passed to the customer

### CPO Vehicles

- [ ] Certified Pre-Owned vehicles display an information sheet with certification details
- [ ] CPO program terms (inspection criteria, warranty coverage) are documented and available to buyers
- [ ] Only vehicles that have actually passed the certification inspection are labeled as CPO

## Multi-Rooftop and Group Compliance

If you manage multiple franchise locations, these items apply group-wide:

- [ ] All stores use the same Buyers Guide form version
- [ ] Addendum templates are standardized across rooftops (or intentionally different per brand per your group's policy)
- [ ] Compliance updates are deployed to all locations simultaneously — not just to whichever manager attends the training
- [ ] There is a single point of contact responsible for monitoring regulatory changes
- [ ] New staff at all locations receive compliance documentation training within their first 30 days

For [franchise dealer groups](/lp/ford-dealers) with multiple rooftops, managing consistency across locations is where most compliance breakdowns happen. The store that didn't get the updated Buyers Guide form is the one that gets cited.

## Staff Training Compliance

- [ ] Sales staff understand they cannot make verbal warranty representations that contradict the As-Is Buyers Guide
- [ ] F&I managers can explain every item on the addendum to a customer on demand
- [ ] Service advisors know not to provide "free repairs" post-sale on As-Is vehicles without documentation
- [ ] Managers understand that verbal promises made during negotiation can create implied warranties

## Documentation Retention

- [ ] Deal jackets include: signed Buyers Guide copy, signed addendum, and all other required disclosures
- [ ] Deal records are retained for the period required by your state (typically 3–5 years)
- [ ] Digital copies are backed up if your record-keeping is paper-based

## Quarterly Review Process

Walk your lot quarterly with this checklist. Specifically:

1. Spot-check 10 random vehicles — do they all have proper Buyers Guides and addendums physically on them?
2. Verify your Buyers Guide form is the current version
3. Pull 5 closed deals and confirm the signed Buyers Guide is in every deal jacket
4. Check that your addendum template matches your current product and pricing lineup

The dealers who avoid FTC issues aren't necessarily doing something complicated. They're just doing the basics consistently — on every vehicle, every time.
